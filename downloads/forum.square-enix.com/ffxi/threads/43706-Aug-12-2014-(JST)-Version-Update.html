    Event-related

    • New Seekers of Adoulin missions have been added.

      • Coalition assignments have undergone the following additions and adjustments.
        A new assignment, “Gather: Ra’Kaznar Inner Court,” has been added.
    • New alter egos have been added.

      Read on for details.
    • A new feature for players to search for quests and NPCs offering tutorial objectives has been added to the following NPCs.
      Ailevia in Southern San d'Oria (I-9) / Matildie in Northern San d'Oria (J-8) / Teilsa in Port San d'Oria (F-9) / Arva in Bastok Mines (H-7) / Reet in Bastok Markets (G-8) / Dulsie in Port Bastok (J-8) / Jack of Hearts in Windurst Waters (north side, G-9) / Jack of Diamonds in Windurst Walls (C-13) / Jack of Clubs in Port Windurst (E-6) / Jack of Spades in Windurst Woods (H-11)
      • Searching for Quests
        1. First, select “I want to find some quests!”
        2. Select the desired area.

          The areas that display will be located in the nation where the NPC resides.
        3. Select an NPC.
        4. The location of the NPC offering the quest will be marked on the map.

          Players not in possession of a map will be introduced to a map vendor.
        • Quests Displayed Using this Feature
          The NPCs invovled will ruminate on players’ fame and main job level and introduce quests that meet those conditions.

          For those quests which do not have a level prerequisite, the NPCs will calculate a recommended level based on the levels at which the quest rewards can be equipped or used, as well as the average level of the monsters inhabiting the areas through which the player must pass.

          * Additional quests are scheduled to be made searchable via this system in future version updates.
      • Searching for Tutorial NPCs
        1. Select “I want to try the tutorial event!”
        2. An NPC providing tutorial objectives will be marked on the map.

          Players not in possession of a map will be introduced to a map vendor.

    • Mog Gardens have undergone the following adjustments.

        Moko Grass / Distilled Water / Lugworm / Little Worm
      • Dung and miracle mulch may now be used as fertilizer for furrows.

      • Monster Rearing has undergone the following adjustments.

        • Under certain conditions, a message will now display in the chat log when feeding a monster something that may greatly speed up its growth.
        • A new monster will no longer succumb to darkness for the first two days (Earth time) after starting to rear it.
        • The requirements for attaining certain Monster Rearing-realted designations have been lowered.
        • Monsters’ mood and degree of satisfaction will now change when feeding them while their growth style is set to “enjoying the moment.”
        • Monsters will no longer change in appearing when feeding themsomething that may greatly speed up their growth is their growth style is set to “enjoying the moment.”
    • Moblin Maze Mongers has undergone the following adjustments.
      • The amount of time that must pass between ordering mazes has been decreased from twenty-four hours (Earth time) to twenty hours.
      • The amount of marbles that must be paid to Duplidoc in Lower Jeuno (H-9) to purchase a maze tabula has been decreased from twenty to five.

    Battle-related

    • New battle content known as “alluvion skirmishes” has been added.



      • About Alluvion Skirmishes
        Alluvion skirmishes are a new type of battle content in which the fiends, bosses, and topographical features change from one labyrinth to the next. Intrepid explorers who meet certain conditions will be able to descend ever deeper into the skirmish. Rewards for vanquishing the enemy leaders include item level 119 weapons and shields and special materials used to engrave them with arcane glyptics.
      • How to Participate
        • Have installed and registered the Seekers of Adoulin expansion pack.
        • Be of level 95 or greater.
        • Be in a party of between one and six players*.
        • The party leader must be in possession of a specific “eudaemon” key item.
          * Alter egos may be called forth in alluvion skirmishes.
      • Forging Into Alluvion Skirmishes
        Oston in Eastern Adoulin (G-8) is able to forge special statues known as “eudamons” using existing simulacra segments—visages, torsos, and legs—by combining them with another newly introduced type. These new segments can be obtained from normal skirmishes.
      • Entering the World of Alluvion Skirmishes
        Alluvion skirmishes utilize the same Augural Conveyors with which pioneers are already familiar. At launch, alluvion skirmishes will be available in Rala Waterways [U] and Cirdas Caverns [U], with more areas scheduled to be added in upcoming version updates.
      • Conquering Alluvion Skirmishes
        Entering an alluvion skirmish via an Augural Conveyor will transport the player’s party into this otherworldly realm, immediately after which the clock will start ticking down.

        Time limits for these skirmishes vary between fifteen and fifty-five minutes, and are determined by the rank of the torso used to construct the corresponding eudaemon. The torso also affects how many floors players are able to attempt.

        Living Cairns situated by the entrance can be used to teleport players to other areas of the skirmish. After entering, conditions such as "vanquish a certain number of enemies," or "defeat a particular notorious monster" will be displayed. These reveal the method for calling forth the skirmish's overlord.

        Vanquishing the leader will reward players with spoils such as item level 119 weapons and shields. Additionally, all party members will receive a “fenestral key” temporary item*. Furthermore, the Living Cairn located in the vicinity will activate, enabling players to progress to further layers of the skirmish.

        * Using a fenestral key will spawn a personal treasure coffer, destroy all Living Cairns, and end the skirmish, giving players a chance to roll lots.
      • Regarding Arcane Glyptics
        Divainy-Gamainy in Western Adoulin (J-10) can engrave weapons and shields earned in alluvion skirmishes with arcane glyptics in exchange for particular items and a fee of obsidian fragments.
        • Materials Required to Engrave Arcane Glyptics
          There are several types of such materials, all of which can be earned in alluvion skirmishes. Although the glyptics engraved by these materials vary in terms of attributes, different items tend to beget particular results, making it possible for players to exert a certain degree of control over what attributes they would like to receive.

    System-related

    • A new feature for registering different combinations of equipment and changing multiple pieces at once, termed “equipment sets,” has been added.
      * For the August version update, up to twenty sets may be registered, and we are considering increasing this number in future version updates.
      • Editing Equipment Sets
        Registering and editing equipment sets can be done via the following subcommands in the “Edit Equip. Set” subsection of the “Macros” category in the main menu.
        • Editing
          The content of an equipment set may be edited in the same way as with the ordinary equipment screen, wherein players register equipment by slot. Players may also set whether a particular piece should remain or be removed.
          Slots designated with “Remove piece” will be marked with an “X”, while those with “Keep same piece” will be left blank.
          • Applicable Equipment
            In order to register pieces of equipment, they must be stored with one of the following systems.
            • Inventory
            • Mog Safe
            • Storage
            • Mog Locker
            • Mog Satchel
            • Mog Sack
            • Mog Case
            • Mog Wardrobe
            *In order to equip arms and armor, they must be stored in either the inventory or the Mog Wardrobe.
            *Augmented, signed, and enchanted items will have their information displayed properly on the item select screen when hovered over, but this is not the case when the item is set.
            *Players possessing a monster in Monstrosity may not register or edit sets, nor change sets using the /equipset text command.
        • Change name
          Changes the name of the equipment set.
        • Copy
          Copies the content of the specified equipment set.
        • Paste
          Pastes the copied content.
        • Delete
          Deletes the specified set.
        • Use Current
          Overwrites the content of the specified set with that of the equipment worn.
      • Changing Equipment Sets
        Players are able to equip different sets by either selecting them from the applicable submenu or with the /equipset command.
        • /equipset [Set Number] [subcommand]
          Equips a designated set of gear.
          >>Set Number (1-20)
          Equips the armor and weapons registered to the specific set.

          >>Subcommand
          echo Displays text that results from executing the command.
          Does not display resultant text if no subcommand is specified.

          For example, to equip gear registered to Set 1, input:
          /equipset 1
      • Stored Set Information
        Items registered to a set and stored in the inventory or Mog Wardrobe will have the name of the storage in which they are placed and the internal location within that storage space displayed in addition to the item name.

        The internal location information will not be lost even if the items within that storage system are sorted. As such, players will be able to designate specific items even if they possess multiple copies of them. However, if the designated item is then moved to another form of storage, the internal location will be lost and a different item of the same name might be equipped instead.

        If an item is moved to a different type of storage after being registered, a different item of the same name will be selected if one exists within the original form of storage, as the program will remember that location.

        If an item of the same name does not exist in that location and the player has registered a piece of equipment from a location other than the inventory or Mog Wardrobe, the program will attempt to equip and item first from the inventory, and then from the Mog Wardrobe.
    • Players may now display the TP of wyverns, avatars, automatons, and beastmasters’ familiars using the text command /partyinfo showtp on.
