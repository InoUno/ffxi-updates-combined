    Battle-related

    • A new advancement system called "Job Points" has been introduced.
      • What Are Job Points?
        Job points are a form of advancement for players who have reached level 99.

        Besting monsters in battle will yield capacity points for a player's main job, and can be converted into job points to further enhance that job.
      • Unlocking Job Points
        Speak with the Nomad Moogle in Ru'Lude Gardens (H-5) after fulfilling the conditions given below in order to receive the "job breaker" key item.
        • Conditions for unlocking job points
          • Be in possession of the "limit breaker" key item
          • Have your main job at level 99
      • Earning Job Points
        Vanquishing monsters of level 96 and above while in possession of the job breaker item will yield capacity points in addition to experience points.

        Players will earn one job point once they have acquired 30,000 capacity points.
        Please note that the amount of capacity points earned varies by monster level and that the number of capacity points earned by a single player is reduced when in a party.
      • Allocating Job Points
        While inside one's Mog House, selecting "Job Points" from the status section of the main menu will provide a list of available jobs. Selecting a job displays a list of categories that may be enhanced.
      • Notes
        • Experience point and capacity point acquisition is reduced when in parties. However, this reduction is lessened in the following areas.
          Sih Gates / Cirdas Caverns / Dho Gates / Woh Gates / Moh Gates
        • Players are unable to earn capacity points from experience points gained by monsters vanquished in Abyssea or reives, by chain bonuses and other bonuses, or by completing certain types of content.
        • Dedication has no effect on the number of capacity points earned.
        • Capacity points exist for each job, and can only be set for one's main job.
    • New Delve Content Has Been Added
      Read on for details on participating.
      • Available Areas
        New Delve content has been added to the following areas.
        Marjami Ravine / Yorcia Weald / Kamihr Drifts

    System-related

    • Transportation devices have been placed at the following coordinates in the lower section of Outer Ra'Kaznar so that players may return to the entrance.
      L-10 / L-6 / D-6 / E-11
    • Players may now peruse the bidding history of posted items before submitting their own to the auction house.
    • Home points have undergone the following adjustments.
      • The following descriptors regarding the location of certain home points have been introduced to the destination menu that appears after selecting on the "Travel to another home point" option and then designating a region.
        • Location Descriptors
          The descriptors are separated into the following three categories:
          • (A) for home points situated near auction houses
          • (E) for home points situated near area exits and entrances
          • (M) for home points situated near Mog Houses
    • The following text commands have been introduced.
      • /lockstyle on/off
        Makes it so that your character's appearance remains the same even after changing equipment.
        >>Subcommands:
        on 　Locks in equipment appearance.
        off 　Removes lock on equipment appearance.
        Displays current setting if no subcommand is specified.
        • Notes
          • This command will be automatically disabled under the following conditions:
            • Zoning in to a different area
            • Equipping items that affect character motions, such as weapons
            • Being the recipient of an attack that removes equipment that influences character motions
            • Logging out
          • This command cannot be enabled while participating in the following content:
            Monstrosity / Ballista / Brenner
          • While the user's appearance will stay the same to those in the area, switching equipment will grant the attributes of the new equipment.
          • The command will remain in effect even when changing into equipment that covers multiple slots.
      • /hidefaith -- /hidetrust
        Disables/enables the display of alter egos not called by the player.
        >>Subcommands:
        on: Disables the display of alter egos.
        off: Enables the display of alter egos.
        • Notes
          This command is intended to lower system usage by disabling the display of other players' alter egos. The following issues will occur, but they are an intended effect of using the command.
          • If a non-displayed alter ego casts a spell on your character, there will be no visual or log notification, but the appropriate status icon will display.
          • If players who have disabled the display of alter egos dismiss one from their parties, the visual effect thereof will not be displayed.
    • Measures have been taken to alleviate freezing during cutscenes for the mission "The Empress Crowned."
