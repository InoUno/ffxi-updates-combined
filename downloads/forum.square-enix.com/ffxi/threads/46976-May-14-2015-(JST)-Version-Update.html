    Event-related

    • The first chapter of Rhapsodies of Vana'diel has been added.
      Players must meet the following prerequisites to commence the scenario.
      • Must be level 3 or greater.
      • Must enter any home nation area connecting a mog house.

        *Once begun, progressing through the scenario will require a registered Rise of the Zilart expansion pack.
    • Key items obtained through progressing through Rhapsodies of Vana'diel will make a wide range of content more accessible, increase skill gains, and further facilitate adventuring in Vana'diel.
      Read on to learn more.
    • A new area, Escha - Zi'Tah, has been added.
      Reaching a certain point in the Rhapsodies of Vana'diel scenario will grant access to Escha - Zi'Tah.
      A wide range of monsters inhabit this area, from the relatively weak creatures that roam the periphery to the high-level and notorious monsters that dwell in its inner reaches.
      • Escha - Zi'Tah currencies
        Defeating monsters in Escha - Zi'tah will yield two kinds of unique currencies.
        1. Escha Silt (maximum of 100,000 pinches)
          Escha silt is acquired by defeating monsters that dwell in Escha - Zi'Tah. It can be used in the following ways:
          Using warp points within Escha - Zi'Tah. / Exchanged for enhancing effects while in Escha - Zi'Tah. / Exchanged for temporary items for use within Escha - Zi'Tah. / Exchanged for key items.
        2. Escha Beads (maximum of 5,000 beads)
          Escha beads are acquired by defeating monsters that dwell in Escha - Zi'Tah. They can be exchanged for materials used to engrave equipment with arcane glyptics.
        * The amount of escha silt and beads that a play may possess at once is scheduled to be increased in future version updates.
      • Boons while in Escha - Zi'Tah
        While in Escha - Zi'Tah, players can take advantage of vorseals, powerful charms that produce a variety of effects.
        • Obtaining vorseals
          Speak to Affi to exchange escha silt for a vorseal.　Upon entering Escha - Zi'Tah the vorseal will automatically radiate its blessing, which will last for one hour (Earth time).
          *The escha silt will only be consumed upon acquiring a vorseal, not when the effect is activated.
          *After one hour has passed or the effect has been manually deactivated, changing areas will reset the timer and reapply the effect.
          　
        • Vorseal types
          New varieties of vorseal will be made available by defeating certain eschan creatures and notorious monsters as well as both advancing to key points in the Rhapsodies of Vana'diel scenario.
      • Temporary items in Escha - Zi'Tah
        A number of temporary items are available for use while in Escha - Zi'Tah.
        • Acquiring temporary items
          Affi offers temporary items in exchange for escha silt. These temporary items will disappear upon leaving the area.
          　
        • Temporary item types
          New temporary items will be made available from defeating certain eschan creatures and notorious monsters as well as by advancing to key points in the Rhapsodies of Vana'diel scenario.

    • Mog Houses have undergone the following adjustments.
      • Mog houses can now be expanded to two stories.
        The following quests must be completed to expand a mog house.
        Growing Flowers / A Lady's Heart / Flower Child
        *If the quests above have already been completed, speaking to the mog house moogle, asking about the second floor, and leaving and reentering the mog house will expand it.
        • Mog house second floor
          The mog house's second floor differs from the first floor in the following ways.
          • Dimensions
            The second floor is slightly larger than the first.
          • Moogle placement
            There is no moogle on the second floor, freeing up space for furnishings.
          • Wall layout
            There are more locations to place wall furnishings. Wall furnishings can now be set to four different heights.
          • Remodeling
            The interior design can be freely changed and set to San d'Orian, Bastokan, or Windurstian architectural styles.
            *Only the second floor can be customized in this way.
        • Going to the second floor
          Select "Change floors." when near your mog house's entrance to move between floors.
          *Player guests cannot change floors this way. To invite a player to the second floor, have them leave the mog house and re-invite them while on the second floor.
        • Mog Safe 2
          Expanding the mog house grants access to Mog Safe 2, a new item storage option.
          *Mog Safe 2 functions identically to Mog Safe.
          *Both Mog Safe and Mog Safe 2 share the same maximum storage value.
        • Supplementary Notes for the Mog House Second Floor
          • Players will be able to enjoy music played by the symphonic curator in the second floor of their Mog House if an orchestrion, Nanaa Mihgo statue, or Nanaa Mihgo statue II is placed there.           • Furnishings that enable the player to proclaim items from moogles, such as the galley kitchen, will continue to have their effects when placed on the second floor.
          • Second floor furnishings will not contribute towards any moghancement effects.
      • While in layout mode, squares where a furnishing can be placed on top of another are now highlighted green.
      • Squares selected in layout mode have been more clearly identified.
      • While in layout mode, the furnishing placement cursor can now be moved in two different ways.
        Type 1: Controlled via the cursor as has been the case previous.
        Type 2: Controlled via the cursor from a top-down perspective regardless of camera location.
      • The camera will now be able to pass through furnishings freely.

      • o The floor number will now be displayed upon entering one's mog house

    Battle-related

    • New battle content, Geas Fete, has been added.
      Geas Fete focuses on battling the notorious monsters that dwell in the new Escha - Zi'Tah area.

      Defeating these notorious monsters will grant the victors experience and capacity points, escha silt and beads—unique currencies specific to Escha—as well as powerful equipment specific to each monster. Adventurers can also find eschalixirs, a mysterious grey liquid used to enhance these items.
      Take on progressively more powerful notorious monsters while obtaining beneficial effects only available in Escha - Zi'Tah.
      • How to participate
        • Form a party of three to eighteen members.
        • Every party member must possess a tribulens key item and the party leader must have a grisly trinket corresponding to the appropriate notorious monster.
        *Alter egos can be called forth for Geas Fete, but they do not count towards the required number of party members.
      • Preparing for battle
        1. Acquiring the first key item
          Speak to Affi in Escha - Zi'Tah (F-10) and exchange escha silt for a tribulens.

          *The amount of silt required to trade for tribulenses will increase with each exchange in eight stages. This increase is tracked for each player individually, and the silt price will slowly revert to its original value in steps after thirty minutes (Earth time) have elapsed.

        2. Acquiring the second key item
          The second "grisly trinket" key item differs based on the notorious monster.
          *Each "grisly trinket" key item bears a different name for its corresponding notorious monster.

          Speak to Affi and select "Receive grisly trinket memorandums" to see a list of each grisly trinket and their prices.

          Exchange silt for the grisly trinket that corresponds to your target notorious monster.
        3. Luring the monsters
          The notorious monster will appear when the party leader inspects the ??? target located in each corner of Escha - Zi'Tah, while in possession of both a tribulens and the appropriate grisly trinket.

          Vanquishing these fearsome foes rewards players with a variety of spoils and escha silt and beads.

          * The key items are consumed upon summoning the monsters.

        4. Augmenting Equipment
          Equipment dropped by the notorious monsters of Escha - Zi'Tah can be engraved with arcane glyptics in stages by exchanging eschalixirs to Nolan in Norg (I-7).

          A preliminary mezzotinting process is performed after one of four different enhancement paths is selected. Mezzotinting does not require any eschalixira, but it does cost silt to perform.

          Applying eschalixir to mezzotinted equipment will enhance its properties through several progressively stronger ranks.

          Item details follow below.

          Type: Selected path.
          Rank: Current equipment enhancement rank.
          NextRP: The number of rank points required before the next rank is reached.

          Equipment dropped by the notorious monsters defeated in Geas Fete can be mezzotinted under a different path while retaining its previous enhancements.

        5. Eschalixirs
          This mysterious grey liquid can extracted from the remains of Escha - Zi'Tah's denizens or bought from Nolan in exchange for escha beads.

          Eschalixirs +1 and +2 can also be obtained from defeating the notorious monsters that roam Escha - Zi'Tah or are engaged in Geas Fete.

          Enhancing equipment with a higher grade eschalixir will grant more rank points than lower quality ones.

    System-related

    • When selecting a subtarget, the subtarget will flash and its nameplate will appear larger. (* Added on May 20, 2015.)
    • A new command to display areas of effect has been added.
      The area of effect display entry in the Config sub-menu has been enabled. Enabling this feature will provide a visual indicator of areas of effect around the target. All targets who will be affected will flash and their nameplates will appear larger. The following types of effects targeting multiple players are compatible.
      Abilities / Magic / Weapon skills / Pet abilities / Enchantments / Items
      * This command is disabled in monstrosity
      * The command will only display circular areas of effect as of this version update. Fan-shaped areas of effect are currently unscheduled.

        Opens up the remodeling section of the Mog House submenu.

      • /furcc [subcommand]
        Sets furniture camera collision options.

        Subcommands:
        on Turns on furniture camera collision.
        off Turns off furniture camera collision.
        Displays current setting if no subcommand is specified.

        Changes control scheme when decorating your Mog House.

        Opens the adventuring primer.
    • A new subcommand, /echo, has been added to the /lockstyleset text command.
      Using /lockstyleset echo will display a message in the log window whenever the command is executed. If a particular equipment set is to be specified, the “echo” subcommand should come after the set number.
      Ex.: /lockstyleset 3 echo
      Attempts to fix appearance to equipment set 3 and displays the results of the command.
